The Five R's of execution infrastructure

Developed by Dr. Minina Johnson of Greater Ambitions LLC. The Five R's are the five operational pillars that determine whether an organization can consistently translate strategy into delivery. Weakness in any single R creates systematic execution failure — regardless of the strength of the others.

R

Anchors the when

Rhythm

The operating cadence — decision cycles and review intervals that keep strategy connected to delivery reality.

No rhythm means strategy dies between meetings. Teams operate on assumptions instead of current intelligence.

R

Anchors the what

Resolution

Decisive prioritization — unambiguous ownership and success criteria at every level of the organization.

Without Resolution, teams execute in different directions. Busy ≠ aligned. Activity ≠ progress.

R

Anchors the with what

Resources

Real availability relative to actual portfolio commitments — not aspirational staffing models.

Most execution failures trace back here. Organizations commit to more than they can deliver — then wonder why delivery fails.

R

Anchors the how it moves

Relay

The structural handoffs where strategy either transfers into action — or breaks down into assumption.

This is where most organizations leak. Strategic intent degrades at every layer it passes through without a Relay architecture.

R

Anchors the what next

Recalibration

The feedback loop that catches drift before it becomes failure — detecting and acting on execution signals early.

Without Recalibration, organizations learn about execution failure at the post-mortem instead of the early-warning.

The Three Moves framework

Original framework developed by Dr. Minina Johnson. These three moves must be executed in order — each one creates the foundation for the next.

Move 01

Own the narrative

Stop letting others define what the PMO is. Reframe every output — status reports, governance reviews, risk registers — as execution intelligence, not administrative compliance. The PMO that controls how its work is interpreted controls how its value is perceived.

Move 02

Design operating infrastructure

Build the connective tissue between strategy and delivery across all Five R's — the rhythms, resolution systems, capacity models, relay architecture, and recalibration loops that allow the organization to execute at scale.

Move 03

Become a strategic capability

Position the PMO as revenue-adjacent — not overhead. Built for leadership decision-making, not reporting to it. The PMO that completes this move becomes irreplaceable. The PMO that does not will be replaced — not by AI, but by leaders who already made the transition.
