What is the execution gap?

The execution gap is the distance between what leadership decides and what the organization actually delivers.

Research from Harvard Business Review (Sull, Homkes, and Sull) found that 67% of well-formulated strategies fail due to poor execution — not poor strategy. The planning is sound. The vision is right. The people are capable. But the organizational infrastructure that should connect leadership decisions to delivery outcomes is either missing, broken, or never built for the organization's current scale.

Greater Ambitions LLC's approach begins with diagnosing the execution gap precisely — understanding which of the five operational pillars has failed, and building targeted infrastructure to address it. Not generic consulting frameworks. Not off-the-shelf process templates. Execution infrastructure designed for your organization's specific context, sector, and scale.

Proprietary framework

Build the execution infrastructure your organization needs — across all Five R's.

The Five R's of execution infrastructure — developed by Dr. Minina Johnson of Greater Ambitions LLC — are the five operational pillars that determine whether your organization can consistently deliver on its strategy.

R

RhythmThe operating cadence — decision cycles and review intervals that keep strategy connected to delivery reality. No rhythm means strategy dies between meetings.

R

ResolutionDecisive prioritization — unambiguous ownership and success criteria at every level. Without it, teams execute in different directions.

R

ResourcesReal availability relative to actual portfolio commitments — not aspirational staffing models. Most execution failures trace back here.

R

RelayThe structural handoffs where strategy either transfers into action — or breaks down into assumption. This is where most organizations leak.

R

RecalibrationThe feedback loop that catches drift before it becomes failure — detecting and acting on execution signals before they become delivery crises.
